Introduction to Magic

In his book ‘Introduction to Magic’, Julius Evola surveys the fundamental concepts and practices of magic from a traditional perspective.

Evola explains the difference between black magic based on selfishness and the desire to control, versus white magic derived from lofty spiritual sources. He also discusses types of traditional magic such as psychic, ritualistic, and theurgic magic.

Evola emphasizes that true magic is not based on superstition but is an exact science requiring comprehensive knowledge of hidden laws of nature and spiritual forces. It also demands self-mastery and profound insight into the nature of reality.

Evola concludes that magic in its lofty sense is an ancient methodology for attaining knowledge and influencing reality, and that properly understanding it requires a traditional methodology.
