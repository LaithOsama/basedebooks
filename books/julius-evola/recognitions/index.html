Recognitions

In Recognitions: Men and Problems, Julius Evola offers reflections on the decline of modern civilization and the need to reconnect with traditional principles. He critically examines contemporary spiritual and political movements from a traditionalist perspective.

A major theme is the loss of any idea of spiritual authority and transcendence in the modern world. Evola laments the rise of materialism, egalitarianism and bourgeois values. He sees this as indicative of a Dark Age of regression from the wisdom of ancient traditions.

Evola rejects left-wing and democratic ideologies as superficial attempts to fill the inner void left by the collapse of tradition. He argues that fascism and nationalism also remain constrained by their modernist tendencies and fail to restore an integral traditional order.

As an alternative, Evola points to the perennial philosophy embodied in teachings like Hermetism, Vedanta and Taoism. By valuing contemplation over action and cultivating detachment, one can preserve principles of spiritual nobility even as outer civilization decays.

Recognitions highlights Evola’s diagnosis of modernity’s ills and his belief that secret esoteric traditions offer means of personal liberation from societal decline.
