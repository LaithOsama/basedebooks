The Path of Cinnabar

“The Path of Cinnabar” outlines Julius Evola’s interpretation of alchemy as a spiritual path. He argues that the key goal of alchemy is inner liberation through access to higher states of consciousness and being.

According to Evola, the alchemical process of transmuting base metals into gold symbolizes the transformation of the ordinary person into an enlightened, immortal adept. This involves harnessing the “fire” within to burn way egoic impurities and uncover the essential Self.

Evola associates key alchemical materials like mercury and cinnabar with psycho-spiritual principles. The Philosopher’s Stone represents the apex of enlightenment and source of the elixir of life. The Magnum Opus or Great Work is the alchemist’s inner journey to spiritual mastery.

While acknowleging fraudulent alchemy, Evola believes true alchemy was reserved for initiated adepts seeking divine knowledge. Alchemical language served to conceal sacred Hermetic teachings from the unprepared.

With its focus on enlightenment and immortality, Evola interprets alchemy as aligned with the Traditionalist goal of overcoming physical existence and acceding to universal, timeless principles.
