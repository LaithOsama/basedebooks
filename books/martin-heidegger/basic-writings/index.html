The Basic Writings

The Basic Writings by philosopher Martin Heidegger provides a gateway into the depths of his profound philosophical ideas. This valuable anthology offers insights into the core themes that preoccupied Heidegger and traces how his thought evolved over a rich intellectual career.

The book includes seminal essays like “On the Essence of Truth” and “The Origin of the Work of Art,” as well as excerpts from his most important work Being and Time, considered one of the most significant philosophical texts of the 20th century. Heidegger wrote in a poetic language that was sometimes obscure, yet imbued with unique meditative power that compels deep reflection.

Heidegger focused on re-posing the fundamental questions of being, truth, and time away from traditional approaches. He tied language, poetry, and art to their ability to express aspects of being that exceed philosophy and science’s grasp. It is a compelling call to contemplate the human relation to being.

The Basic Writings is arguably the best introduction to Heidegger’s philosophy that changed the course of modern thought, and continues to spur thinkers to reconsider many assumptions about the nature of being and knowledge.
