Poetry, Language, Thought

In his book ‘Poetry, Language, Thought’, Martin Heidegger explores the essential relationship between poetry, language, and thought. For him, poetry is not merely an aesthetic art, but a unique way of language that opens new horizons for thinking and revealing meanings of being.

Heidegger sees poetry as enabling language to manifest itself in its original form. In poetry, language does not become just a tool for practical communication, but reaches the essence of itself as a means of uncovering truth. Poetry also has the power to directly address being and evoke a sense of wonder and astonishment before the mystery of being.

Thus, Heidegger argues for poetry’s ability to express the truth of being in ways that logical thinking and traditional philosophy cannot reach. For him, poetry represents an authentic thinking able to approach being and reveal aspects of it that other forms of thought cannot.
