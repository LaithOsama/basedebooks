Fundamental Concepts of Metaphysics

In his Fundamental Concepts of Metaphysics, Martin Heidegger surveys the basic concepts of metaphysics from his perspective. Through this work, Heidegger aims to elucidate his vision of the nature of metaphysics and its relation to the question of being.

Heidegger discusses concepts such as being and beings, nothingness, becoming, time, and reality. He also examines traditional metaphysical categories like unity, truth, goodness, and beauty.

Heidegger emphasizes that metaphysics in its essence is concerned with questioning the “meaning of being.” As such, Heideggerian metaphysics is not interested in describing things, but in seeking the meaning behind their existence.

This concise work provides a unique window into understanding Heidegger’s radical redefinition of metaphysics away from the traditional conception of it as merely a “science of things transcendent.”
