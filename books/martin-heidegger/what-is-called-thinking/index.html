What Is Called Thinking?

In his book What is Called Thinking?, Martin Heidegger inquires into the essence of thinking and how it relates to the search for truth. He criticizes the traditional view of thinking as merely a tool for solving problems, and calls for a deeper understanding of thinking as a way to approach being.

Heidegger sees authentic thinking as a continual contemplation of being, not just a means to achieve specific goals. For him, thinking is an experience of encountering being and attempting to grasp the meaning of truth.

He also argues that language is integral to thinking, as thinking manifests through language. Yet thinking transcends logic and rationality toward broader horizons of awareness and uncovering meaning.

Heidegger calls for a thinking that goes beyond the familiar and opens to wonder and astonishment before the mystery of being. For him, thinking is the only way to awaken humankind from the forgetfulness of being, and return to perpetual questioning about the meaning of life.

Through this book, Heidegger establishes a novel, authentic understanding of the nature of thinking as existential contemplation, as opposed to the narrow view of thinking merely as a tool to achieve specific ends.
