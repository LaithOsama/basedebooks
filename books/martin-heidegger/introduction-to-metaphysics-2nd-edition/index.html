Introduction to Metaphysics

In his Introduction to Metaphysics, Martin Heidegger lays out the central themes of his philosophy on being and metaphysics. Through this work, Heidegger seeks to move beyond the traditional view of metaphysics and propose a new conception focused on the question of being.

Heidegger proceeds from questioning and wonder before the essence of being and the means of comprehending it. He sees the task of metaphysics as inquiring into the meaning of being rather than merely describing reality, since being transcends beings.

Heidegger also criticizes the rationalist and empiricist tendencies that have dominated Western thought, considering them incapable of grasping the truth of being. Instead, he calls for existential contemplation that opens broader horizons for understanding human experience.

Introduction to Metaphysics represents a seminal work for grasping the turning point Heidegger brought about in contemporary philosophical thought by re-posing the question of the nature of being and the possibility of knowing it.
