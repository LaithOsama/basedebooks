The Geopolitical Future of Russia

In his book Foundations of Geopolitics, Russian thinker Alexander Dugin presents his vision for geopolitics, proposing a new concept of international order based on civilizational pluralism.

Dugin criticizes traditional geopolitical notions based on the nation-state, calling for a multipolar world order recognizing the differentiation of civilizations and large geographical identities.

Dugin also sees Russia as located at the “heart” of Eurasia, and hence should play a pivotal role in shaping the new geopolitical order through alliances with other Eurasian powers like China and Germany.

Dugin’s ideas around the plurality of civilizational realms represent an attempt to move beyond traditional geopolitical views based on the nation-state concept.
