Political Platonism

In his book Political Platonism: The Philosophy of Politics, Russian thinker Alexander Dugin draws on the ancient Greek philosopher Plato to present a new vision for political philosophy that transcends modern ideologies.

Dugin delves into the Platonic idea of the philosopher-king, who possesses both intellectual wisdom and the moral integrity to guide the state. He draws parallels between Plato’s concept of the ideal ruler and the challenges of leadership in modern society, urging a return to a more principled and visionary form of governance.

The book explores the concept of the “Philosopher-Strategist,” a term Dugin coins to describe a leader who combines philosophical insight with practical political acumen. He argues that a true political leader must have a deep understanding of metaphysics and ethics to navigate the complexities of power and to guide the state towards the pursuit of the highest good.

Dugin’s treatise also engages with the concept of the “Eternal Idea,” which he interprets as an underlying truth that transcends historical contingencies. He emphasizes the importance of aligning political systems with eternal and transcendent principles to ensure their sustainability and harmony with the cosmic order.

Dugin builds on Plato’s concept of the virtuous state ruled by philosopher-kings, calling for the revival of this principle in the modern era. He also adopts Plato’s idea of dividing society into classes based on individuals’ qualifications and talents rather than equality.

Dugin sees such a Platonic system as renewing political thought by combining traditional wisdom and proper spiritual leadership. For him, Platonism is the way to establish a political order derived from metaphysical principles instead of materialist ideologies.

In the realm of governance. Dugin’s synthesis of ancient wisdom and contemporary political discourse offers a compelling case for a more enlightened and spiritually grounded approach to political philosophy.
