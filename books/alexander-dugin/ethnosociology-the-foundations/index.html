Ethnosociology: The Foundations

Alexander Dugin

philosophy politics

“a sociological framework that examines the role of ethnicity and culture in shaping social structures and dynamics.”

view in arabic download section

In his book Ethnosociology: The Foundations, Russian thinker Alexander Dugin proposes a novel approach to the social sciences focused on studying civilizational and cultural identities rather than individuals or societies.

Dugin puts forth the term “ethnosociology” to describe this new methodology that views ethnic groups or civilizational collectives as living entities with their own particularities. He sees humans as impossible to understand in isolation from the civilizational and cultural context they belong to.

Dugin also emphasizes the imperative to respect the diversity and plurality of cultures and identities, rejecting the universalist tendency to impose a single cultural model.
