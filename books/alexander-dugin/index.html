Alexander Dugin

Alexander Dugin is a prominent Russian philosopher, political analyst, and author. Born on January 7, 1962, in Moscow, Russia, Dugin has made significant contributions to the field of political philosophy and is best known for his influential geopolitical theories. His ideas have stirred both admiration and controversy, as he presents a unique worldview that challenges conventional political and philosophical paradigms.

Autobiography

Alexander Dugin’s intellectual journey began during his university years at Moscow State University, where he studied philosophy and graduated with a degree in sociology. His academic pursuits exposed him to a diverse range of philosophical traditions, including Western philosophies such as German Idealism, Existentialism, and Traditionalism, as well as Eastern philosophies like Hinduism and Buddhism.

Dugin’s early encounters with these various philosophical systems left a deep impression on him, shaping his distinctive approach to philosophy and geopolitics. Over the years, he became a prolific writer, publishing numerous books and essays that explored his philosophical ideas and political theories.

Traditionalism and Geopolitics

From a traditional philosophical standpoint, Alexander Dugin’s ideas draw inspiration from the works of prominent Traditionalist thinkers such as René Guénon and Julius Evola. Traditionalism posits that there is a transcendent spiritual truth underlying all the major world religions and philosophies, which can serve as a unifying principle for humanity.

Dugin synthesizes Traditionalism with his geopolitical theories, creating a framework that he calls “Eurasianism.” This concept advocates for a multipolar world order in which various civilizations, particularly the Eurasian landmass, maintain their unique identities and cultural autonomy. According to Dugin, this multipolar system opposes the dominance of Western liberal democracy and promotes a more inclusive and diverse global society.

Dugin’s geopolitical theories are based on the idea that geopolitical spaces hold a significant influence over political and social developments. He argues that the struggle for geopolitical dominance is a fundamental aspect of human history and that different civilizations possess distinct geopolitical identities and interests.

In his seminal work, “Foundations of Geopolitics: Geopolitical Future of Russia,” Dugin outlines a strategy for Russia to assert its geopolitical power and influence. He suggests that Russia should aim to build alliances and strengthen its presence in various regions, such as Central Asia, the Middle East, and Europe, in order to challenge the global dominance of the United States and its Western allies.
