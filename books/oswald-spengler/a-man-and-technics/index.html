Man and Technics

In his book ‘Man and Technics’, historian and philosopher Oswald Spengler sees technology not just as an external set of tools, but an integral part of a civilization’s own spirit. Societies develop technologies that reflect their intellectual and spiritual worldview.

Spengler warns against falling prey to “progress” in technology without awareness of its nature. Technology transforms humans and alters our outlook on life in ways we may not realize. Blind reliance on technology threatens to commodify life and lose the spiritual dimension.

Thus Spengler calls for a deeper understanding of technology’s nature and impact, and the need to uphold genuine humanistic and cultural values in the face of rapid technological advancement.
