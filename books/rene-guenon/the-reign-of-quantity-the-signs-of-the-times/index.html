The Reign of Quantity and the Signs of the Times

René Guénon

philosophy religion politics

“a philosophical and metaphysical work that analyzes the state of the modern world and its underlying spiritual crisis. Guénon explores the concept of “quantity” as a dominant principle in the modern age, which leads to a decline in qualitative values and the fragmentation of knowledge”

A profound and thought-provoking work by the eminent metaphysical philosopher René Guénon. this book offers a penetrating analysis of the modern world’s intellectual and spiritual decline and its preoccupation with materialistic values and quantitative thinking.

Guénon begins by critiquing the dominant trends of the modern age, such as scientific materialism, relativism, and the worship of quantity over quality. He contends that the modern world has lost touch with its spiritual heritage, leading to a crisis in understanding the true nature of reality and the purpose of human existence.

The book explores the consequences of the modern focus on quantitative analysis, which has led to a shallow and superficial understanding of reality. Guénon contrasts this with the traditional perspective, rooted in metaphysical principles, which acknowledges the importance of qualitative and spiritual dimensions in the understanding of existence.

Another critical theme in the book is the concept of “traditional metaphysics,” which represents a timeless and universal knowledge that transcends cultural and historical boundaries. Guénon argues that the fragmentation of knowledge in the modern world has led to the loss of this traditional wisdom, resulting in a deep crisis of meaning and purpose.

Throughout the book, Guénon warns against the dangers of the prevailing materialistic and reductionist worldview and calls for a return to traditional wisdom and spirituality. He urges individuals to recognize the signs of the times and seek higher truths beyond the limitations of empirical and quantitative knowledge.
