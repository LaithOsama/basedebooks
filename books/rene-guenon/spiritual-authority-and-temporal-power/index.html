Spiritual Authority and Temporal Power

In Spiritual Authority and Temporal Power, René Guénon elucidates the relationship between spiritual and temporal authority in traditional civilizations. He argues that legitimate temporal power derives from spiritual principles enshrined in tradition.

According to Guénon, temporal power in traditional societies issues from spiritual authority. Secular rulers governed by divine right, guided by principles of sacred kingship and a metaphysical order. This maintained an equilibrium between the spiritual elite and the temporal order.

Guénon asserts that modern Western civilization has inverted this relationship, subordinating the sacred to the temporal power of the state. The severing of political authority from spiritual principles is the source of the profound disorder in the modern world.

Guénon advocates restoring the connection between temporal and spiritual authority. Legitimate government must be re-established on the basis of intellectual and sacred principles transmitted from above, not popular consent from below.

With its call to re-found politics on Traditional authority, Spiritual Authority and Temporal Power remains a foundational exposition of Guénon’s idea of metaphysically constituted society.
