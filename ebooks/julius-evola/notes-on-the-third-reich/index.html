Notes on the Third Reich

Julius Evola

politics, history, philosophy.

In Notes on the Third Reich, Julius Evola offers a critical examination of National Socialism in Germany. He argues that while the Third Reich opposed aspects of modernity, it failed to completely dismantle liberalism and revive a sacred traditional order.

Evola praises the Third Reich’s rejection of bourgeois values and promotion of heroism and hierarchy. However, he believes Nazi ideology retained modernist elements of rationalism, idealism and materialism that diluted its traditionalist potential.

According to Evola, the Third Reich did not institute a radically anti-egalitarian society as he wished. Its socialism and totalitarianism contained modern collectivist aspects. Its racism also lacked a truly esoteric foundation.

Evola considers the Third Reich a missed opportunity to restore the Holy Roman Empire ideal. By compromising with modernity, the Nazi regime failed to lead a spiritual rehabilitation of the West guided by ancient Indo-Aryan principles.

With its critique of National Socialism’s limitations, Notes on the Third Reich distinguishes Evola from fascists by underscoring his uncompromising adherence to Tradition and opposition to the modern world.
