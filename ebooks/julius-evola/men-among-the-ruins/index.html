Men Among the Ruins: Post-War Reflections of a Radical Traditionalist

Julius Evola

philosophypolitics spirituality

“a philosophical and political work that critiques the modern world and proposes an alternative vision for a traditional, hierarchical society.”

view in arabic download section

“Men Among the Ruins” outlines Evola’s vision for the radical right-wing transformation of modern society inspired by ancient Roman and European Traditions.

Evola argues that the West has declined from the unity, hierarchy and nobility of its Imperial past. He rejects liberal democracy, socialism and egalitarianism as “antitraditions” undermining heroic elitism.

To revive the glories of Tradition, Evola calls for an authoritarian political system led by a spiritual elite. This Order would establish an anti-democratic, anti-materialist State and society dedicated to conquering the inner, worldly limitations of humans. Its spirit is that of an “aristocracy of character and spirituality.”

Evola believes the new man of the West must reject the corrupted modern world, and through asceticism and adherence to Tradition, become a spiritual warrior-hero who overcomes internal and external limitations. This will reverse the decline of the West and forge a new heroic, spiritual civilization.

While controversial in its disdain for egalitarianism, “Men Among the Ruins” is a seminal work of counter-modern Traditionalism that rejects liberal democracy and egalitarianism.
