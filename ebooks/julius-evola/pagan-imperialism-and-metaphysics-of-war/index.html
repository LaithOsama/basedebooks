Pagan Imperialism and the Metaphysics of War

Julius Evola

philosophy politics spirituality

“explores the historical and cultural aspects of paganism and its connection to imperialistic endeavors.”

view in arabic download section

“Pagan Imperialism” outlines Julius Evola’s early political philosophy which combines his Traditionalist beliefs with support for an imperial order. The book criticizes Christian morals as emasculating Rome and advocates a return to the pagan Imperial Roman spirit.

According to Evola, Christian ethics of love, equality and compassion weakened the Roman Empire by undermining its warrior ethos and hierarchical structure. He calls for replacing Christian morality with pagan Roman values like heroism, conquest and social stratification.

Evola argues that the Catholic Church’s suppression of Roman paganism was illegitimate. He claims early Christianity infiltrated Rome surreptitiously, justifying its rise through compromised emperors and Church forgeries like the Donation of Constantine.

In contrast to democratic and socialist ideas, Evola asserts that Imperium - sacred imperial sovereignty - should be the political organizing principle. He admires the totalitarian impulse in Fascism and believes spiritual transcendence can be realized through an imperial system combining authority and hierarchy.

While controversial for its dismissal of Christianity and embrace of imperialism, “Pagan Imperialism” outlines Evola’s early attempts to merge Traditionalism with an inegalitarian vision of political order inspired by ancient Rome.
