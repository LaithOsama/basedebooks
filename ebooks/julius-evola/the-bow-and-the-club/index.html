The Bow and the Club

In Bow and Club, Julius Evola outlines his philosophy of politics from a traditionalist perspective. He argues that modern democratic politics has lost connection to traditional principles of authority and spiritual order.

Evola associates the “Bow” with contemplation, introspection and inner mastery. This represents the higher ruling principles derived from metaphysical Tradition. The “Club” symbolizes the external means of exerting material power and control.

According to Evola, traditional civilizations understood the primacy of the Bow over the Club. The spiritual nobility ruled over secular affairs through sacred authority rather than brute force. But modern secular politics inverts this relationship, making power and coercion the Supreme governing principles.

Evola advocates embracing Tradition’s wisdom to restore balance between man’s inner qualities and external capabilities. This means renouncing materialism and re-orienting politics to conform to higher, transcendent truths instead of physical realities.

Through its call to re-connect politics to sacred Tradition, Bow and Club promotes Evola’s vision of an anti-modern spiritual Imperium governed by timeless values.
