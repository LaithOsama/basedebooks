In Symbolism of the Cross, René Guénon explicates the universal metaphysical significance of the cross symbol across various sacred traditions. He argues the cross represents the manifestation of spiritual realities in the cosmic order.

According to Guénon, the vertical axis of the cross symbolizes the connection between Heaven and earth while the horizontal axis represents the extension of the spiritual principle into the manifested world.

The cross epitomizes the descent of spirit into matter to order and vivify creation. It embodies metaphysical relationships like the axis linking the Principle and Manifestation, the Universal Man, the Tree of Life, and more.

Guénon describes the cross as a synthesis of opposing dualities - vertical and horizontal, spirit and matter. By integrating dualisms, the cross reflects the constitution of the universe rooted in primordial unity.

With its metaphysical explication of the cross, Guénon highlights a ubiquitous traditional symbol that distills cosmic order, spiritual essence, and metaphysical realities.
