The Question Concerning Technology and Other Essays

Martin Heidegger

philosophy

“examines the essence of technology and its impact on humanity and the world. He critiques the modern view of technology, and calls for a more contemplative approach to technology that respects its essence and its effects on human existence.”

In his essay collection ‘The Question Concerning Technology and Other Essays’, Martin Heidegger examines the relationship between humankind and modern technology, and its impact on how we view the world.

In his renowned essay ‘The Question Concerning Technology’, Heidegger criticizes the prevalent notion of technology as a neutral tool that humans can make use of however they want. Instead, he argues technology has come to form a ‘framework’ that determines how we look at the world.

Other essays like ‘The Age of the World Picture’ and ‘The Question Concerning the Thing’ discuss how modern science and the dominance of technology shape our conception of nature and reality, warning against the ‘forgetfulness of Being’ behind technological progress.

Through these essays, Heidegger tries to highlight the potential dangers of technology’s supremacy, and call upon humankind to deeply ponder our relationship with technology instead of seeing it as merely neutral tools.
